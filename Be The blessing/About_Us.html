About Company Page


Being a Blessing is for self empowerment and external development


BTB seeks to create impact through communal activism and inspiring individuals (young and old) to make an impact through action. Our focus areas are driven by our family-oriented model of providing access for all and supporting those most challenged to support the overall well-being of the community. This mean BTB will actively grow and build programs that help promote and sustain the socio-economic status of native (or historically affluent/populous) individuals within the community.


Our Values


MISSION: Be The Blessing Foundation (BTB) will bring resources and programs exposing and encouraging opportunity and growth to underrepresented and underprivileged communities, while gradually diminishing the impacts of disparities.


In partnership with DC SYEP, MD SYEP and Precision Construction Company for development and mentorship opportunities across metropolitan areas.

Meet the Team

Jay Cammon

Jays Bio Placeholder Valedictorian Student-athlete of the Friendship Collegiate Academy Class of 2015, Jay graduated as a student-athlete at the University of Pennsylvania Class of 2019, studying Environmental Studies and minoring in Economic Policy. At Penn, served as a Perry World House Fellow studying Energy resilience in Africa and a mentor in the Beat the Streets Philadelphia mentor program. Played 4 years of collegiate football obtaining numerous All-Ivy Honors and awards. Currently works for ExxonMobil as a Product Lifecycle Manager for Data , previous serving as a global business advisor. Founded Be the Blessing Foundation and serves as Director of Business Development with NEX^ Foundation with fellow alumni Brian White (Founder). Jay enjoys fellowship and supporting entrepreneurial interest of his peers. While drawing closer to his ultimate goal of enabling small business and energy resilience in under privileged and underrepresented communities, Jay enjoys connecting history and cultural dynamics to present day challenges while obtaining distinct experiences. Jay enjoys traveling, motocross sports, fishing, cooking and horses. Jay seeks to be a trend curator and problem solver.

Ajai Cammon

Ms. Ajai Cammon has recently graduated from Wake Forest University (WFU) and earned a Bachelor of Science in Health and Exercise and a minor in Neuroscience. She is originally from the D.C. metropolitan area and graduated from Duke Ellington School of the Arts in Washington, D.C. with a focus in dance. Ms. Cammon 's talents have allowed her opportunities to perform within professional and notable international dance companies to include Alvin Ailey, Dance Theatre of Harlem and Hubbard Street Chicago. Studying almost every genre of dance, she has a passion for life and loves to use her art form as a means of communication and self-care which allows her the ability to educate and improve the quality of life of others

Ajai's professional experience ranges from local government and human resources to scientific research and physical therapy. Conducting research at Wake Forest Baptist Health, Ajai has been a part of a National Institutes of Health published science intervention using dance to tackle the symptoms and adverse effects of Dementia/Alzheimer's disease. Ms. Cammon was previously the Vice President of Autism Speaks Chapter at WFU, Executive Content Creator for an African American women's organization known as The Sister's Circle and participated as a member of the Wake Forest University's Dance Team. Currently, Ms. Cammon has kick started her career in the healthcare industry as a Client Solutions Associate with the esteemed Gerson Lehrman Group. In her free time she enjoys cooking, dancing and serving her community through outreach programs. Moving along track in her career and B.S. in Health and Exercise, Ajai plans to use all of her knowledge and exposure to help those that may not be able to help themselves.
